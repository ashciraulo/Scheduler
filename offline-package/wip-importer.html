How matching works. A row is proposed when any include keyword appears in the Description column. Job Task Description is not searched — in BC it holds progress text like "WIP 50%" rather than what the work is — but it is carried through to the review table and the export notes. Exclusions override includes. Combination rules sit between the two: when every word in a rule appears in the same Description, that job is held back and listed under Not matched tagged "needs review" — so a job that is yours when it says "body", and yours when it says "elbow", but something else when it says both, lands in front of you rather than being guessed at. Duplicates are detected on Job No. — the row with the most complete data is kept and the rest are set aside, not deleted. Everything is reviewable above, and nothing is exported unless it is ticked.

Actual Completion Date is only trusted when corroborated. Business Central has been observed filling this column on jobs that have not started, so a date alone is never enough. A job counts as finished — and is unticked automatically — only when a completion date appears together with a status of ready for dispatch, ready for invoicing, finance to invoice or complete. A date with any other status is flagged for you to check, and a date on an on order job is called out as a direct contradiction. Nothing is ever hidden — unticked jobs stay visible and tickable, and the count appears in the summary above. Every job still exports as active, with the BC date recorded in notes marked unverified.

Where your settings live. Your keyword lists and combination rules are saved in this browser's local storage — nothing else is stored, and no WIP data is ever written to disk. That storage is tied to this browser on this machine, and browsers can clear it (or lose track of it when the file moves), so use Save settings to file to keep a copy or carry your setup to another browser or computer, and Load settings from file at the other end. The settings file contains only keywords and rules, so it is safe to share or store.

Department value. Business Central's Total Contract Value (LCY) is the value of the whole job to the company, not your department's share. The export carries it through as totalValue and leaves departmentValue at 0 for you to fill in — the scheduler's value report depends on that distinction, so it is not guessed here.
